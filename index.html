2. Types of Data Processed

Fooser is a free Android app without in-app purchases and without the need to create a user account. No sensitive personal data is collected.

Anonymized Usage Statistics:

In order to improve the app and better adapt it to the needs of the users, we use Firebase Analytics to collect anonymized usage statistics. These statistics include, for example, information about:

  • App crashes
  • App start times
  • Functions used
  • Interactions within the app

The collection of this data is optional for the user. You can choose in the app settings whether this data collection should take place or not. If you deactivate the collection, no usage statistics will be collected.

3. Legal Basis of Processing

The processing of your anonymized usage data is based on your consent (Art. 6 para. 1 lit. a GDPR). You have the right to revoke your consent at any time by deactivating the data collection in the app settings. The revocation of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out on the basis of consent until revocation.

4. Disclosure of Data to Third Parties

The anonymized data collected as part of Firebase Analytics is transmitted to Google. Google may use this data to provide and improve its services. For more information on data processing by Google, please see Google's privacy policy:

Google Privacy Policy

Otherwise, your data will not be disclosed, sold or otherwise disclosed to third parties, unless this is required by law.

5. Data Security

We take appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your data from unauthorized access, loss or misuse. Since the data collected is anonymized usage statistics, the risk of data misuse is low. However, we would like to point out that data transmission over the Internet can involve security risks.

6. International Data Transfer

The data collected by Firebase Analytics may be transferred to Google servers in the USA and other countries outside the European Union. Google is certified under the EU-US Data Privacy Framework. More information about that you can find here: EU-US Data Privacy Framework

7. Your Rights as a User

As a user, you have the following rights:

  • Right of access (Art. 15 GDPR): You have the right to obtain information about the data we process. Since we only process anonymized data, it is generally not possible for us to assign this data to a specific person.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16 GDPR): You have the right to request the correction of incorrect data. Since we only process anonymized data, a correction is generally not possible.
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17 GDPR): You have the right to request the deletion of your data. Since you can deactivate the collection of data in the app settings, this is equivalent to deletion.
  • Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR): You have the right to request the restriction of the processing of your data. Since we only process anonymized data and you can deactivate the collection, a restriction is generally not necessary.
  • Right to object (Art. 21 GDPR): You have the right to object to the processing of your data. You can object to the processing of your data by deactivating the data collection in the app settings.
  • Right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (Art. 77 GDPR): You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority if you believe that the processing of your data violates the GDPR.

8. Changes to this Privacy Policy

We reserve the right to change this privacy policy to adapt it to changed legal requirements or technical circumstances. The updated privacy policy will be published in the app and on our website. It is recommended that you review this privacy policy regularly.
